"To Kill a Mockingbird" is a classic novel written by Harper Lee. Set in the American South during the 1930s, the story unfolds through the perspective of Scout Finch, a young girl, and follows her father, Atticus Finch, a lawyer defending a black man, Tom Robinson, accused of raping a white woman. The narrative explores themes of racial injustice, moral growth, and compassion, providing a poignant commentary on the deeply ingrained prejudices within society. The book's title symbolizes the senseless harm inflicted on innocent beings, emphasizing the importance of empathy and understanding.
